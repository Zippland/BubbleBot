Search for a person in the current group chat by name to get an @-mention marker.

Use this when you want to @-mention someone in your reply but don't yet know their channel-native ID. Returns matching members along with their `<@id>` markers. Embed the marker directly into your reply text — the channel layer will translate it into a real @-mention on send.

Only works in group chats; private chats / CLI return "No group members available." The search is case-insensitive substring match against display names. If multiple people match, refine the query (e.g., longer fragment).

Example usage:
- You want to reply to a Bob who asked something. Call find_person("Bob") → returns `Bob → <@wxid_xxx>`. Your reply: `好的 <@wxid_xxx> 我看下`.

Do NOT call this when:
- You're not in a group chat (the tool will tell you, but it's wasted call).
- You already see a `<@id>` marker in the conversation history — just reuse it directly.
- The person hasn't appeared in this group's roster (tool can't find them).
